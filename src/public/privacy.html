Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

1. Information We Collect

Autobug collects the following types of information:

  • Account Information: Email address, company name, and role when you register.
  • Ticket Data: Issue descriptions and screenshots you submit for AI processing.
  • Repository Data: File paths and code snippets from your connected repositories that are sent to AI models for analysis.
  • Technical Data: IP address, browser type, and timestamps for security and audit purposes.

2. How We Use Your Information

  • To process and resolve your support tickets using AI-assisted code generation.
  • To authenticate you and manage access to your account.
  • To maintain security, prevent fraud, and comply with legal obligations.
  • To improve our AI models and service quality (anonymized data only).

3. AI Subprocessors

To provide our service, Autobug sends certain data to third-party AI providers:

  • Groq: Ticket descriptions and technical context are sent to Groq for context analysis and prompt enhancement. Groq Privacy Policy
  • Ollama (self-hosted): Code files and prompts are processed on our self-hosted Ollama infrastructure. Data does not leave our controlled environment.

We have signed Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) with all AI subprocessors where required.

4. Data Retention

  • Tickets and images are retained for 90 days after resolution, unless you request earlier deletion.
  • Account data is retained while your account is active. You may request deletion at any time.
  • Audit logs are retained for 1 year for security and compliance.

5. Your Rights (GDPR / CCPA)

  • Access: Request a copy of your personal data.
  • Deletion: Request deletion of your account and associated data.
  • Portability: Export your ticket history.
  • Correction: Update inaccurate account information.

To exercise these rights, contact us at privacy@autobug.com.

6. Security

We implement industry-standard security measures including:

  • AES-256 encryption for sensitive configuration data at rest.
  • TLS 1.3 for all data in transit.
  • Role-based access control (RBAC) with audit logging.
  • Regular security assessments and penetration testing.
